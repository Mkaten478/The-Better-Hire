Understanding the Interview Process

During this stage of finding employment, you are set to present your best self and prove you're worthy of the postiion. You must be dressed accordingly to give an exceptional first impression. Remember to be respectful and use proper grammer when speaking since you are being compared to the other applicants you must find a way to stand out!

Finding Your Desired Job

The internet is your friend! Think of the career path you would like to explore and search online for listings.

Online listings:

  • Indeed.com
  • Ziprecruiter.com
  • Monster.com
  • EmploymentGuide.com
In any online search, take advantage of keywords and tags. Most sites have a decent filtration process so you can check off what kind of requirements you have and see only results that fit those restraints.

If you are unsure as to what profession you'd like to enter, complete a career aptitude test based on what you'd most likely suceed at, or another test to find the job position fitting your own requirements.

Once you've compiled your options, think if there's any way to get trial work for it since the only way you'll know if you enjoy something is to try it. Do not be discouraged if at first you don't succeed since every skill takes practice to master. There are even courses to help build your skill set if that's what the job requires.

Preparing Yourself for the Job

This is detailing how to dress formally, some interviews call for business casual attire, but unless stated otherwise, assume you must dress professionally. It is best to be overdressed than underdressed.

Even when working on a budget, one can find great items to assemble a clean, appropriate look. You can find tips for how to spend your money wisely. Men are expected to wear a standard suit in a solid color- such as black, grey or navy - with little to no jewelry and a neat hairstyle. Let the employer know you can take care of yourself well, so you can apply that in whatever way to your job.

Women have many more options when it comes to professional dress, but the key is to make sure the colors match. For reference, use the color wheel to find complimentary colors. Women have the option of suits, as well as skirts paired with a blouse, or conservative dress.

Professionalizing Your Media

Go through your public social media accounts and get rid of posts which may portray you in a negative light. Your potential employer will likely do a background check to see what kind of person you are outside of the office and the best way to do so that many others have implemented is searching through private social media accounts.

Stay clear of:

  • Profanity
  • Scandalous pictures
  • Hateful comments on other's posts

You are working to portray your business side, but as long as you work for someone, you are a representation of the company, even if you don't realize it.

Especially be careful posting about your work as your boss may stumble upon it and see your true feelings. If you have any griefs with your job, keep them off the internet where nothing disappears completely.

On the other hand, you can moderately flaunt your interests and skills online to prove what you stated as your strengths was not merely a bluff solely to get the job. If you complete a project you're proud of, feel free to post about it and include links, if possible.

Familiarizing Yourself with the Company

Before going into the interview, make sure you know atleast a basic background on the company to display commitment.

Finding out:

  • The founding of the company
  • The purpose
  • The services they offer, if applicable

Will help in that you can adapt your speaking points to comment on what you can do for them.

Preparing Resumé & Cover Letter

I'm sure you're aware first impressions matter and your cover letter will be the first piece of you they get to know. If you're able to catch their attention in that then they move onto your resume to learn more about you. The way in which you format things is very telling, and any potential employer will note if all the information is clustered together and hard to read, or, more positively, if the layout is aesthetically pleasing.

Presenting your best professional self not only applied to clothing, but also to the appearance of your writing. Showcase what you believe would make someone want you on their team.

In any case with writing you must know your audience, and in this instance, that means writing formally; so refrain from using abbreviations or shortening words into a single letter. Example of what not to do: "u kno I'm the best, so r u gonna hire me alredee".

Resumé

Your resume is meant to be a brief summary of your education, qualifications and previous experiences.

Tips for writing your resume:

  • Make strategic use of bold, caps, and italic
  • Balance white space
  • Have the best information in the top third section of the page since that is what is first viewed
  • Tailor your resume to the job description
  • Use attractive, readable fonts such as Arial, Verdana, and Helvetica

Interview Preparation

Although all interviews differ, they have some of the same expectations and traditions that you should anticipate and practice for before hand. First, you should know what type of interview you will be partaking in, whether it be standar one-on-one interview, a panel interview, a stress interview, etc.

Next, start preparing for the physical aspect of the interview. This includes what you look like such as your outfit, hairstyle, hygeine, and jewelry, as well as your interactions. Work on maintaining eye contact, a firm handshake, body posture, and preventing bad habits like shaking or biting your lip. Also, understand how you are going to go about getting there; what route are you going to take, when is an appropriate time to leave in order to be there 10 minutes early, and how are you going to avoid any other mistakes or disasters.

On the other hand, you will also need to prepare for the mental content of the interview that they will examine most. You need to show that you have what it takes to be an employee, thus you should be prepared to answer any question they throw at you. There are plenty of questions that almost any interviewer will ask, these you should have down and be ready to answer on command. But, you should also prepare to answer questions related to the position and your fullfilment of that position.

Also, write down some questions to ask the interviewer. Yes, they are the ones who should be asking the questions, but this will eliminate the chance of making a one-sided interview and it shows that you have more interest in the position.

Behavior

Behavior relates to your actions during the interview, obviously you want to be friendly and kind, but there is more to it than just that.

Body language makes up most of a person's communication, thus you should be cautious of yours and keep it in check. Keep your back straight and chin up, but don't look too intimidating. When sitting feel free to lean in it will give off the impression that you are more intersted.

Your vocabulary should also be customized for the occasion. Most people use profanity, but you have to know when to limit it, and during the interview this is absolutely unwanted. Also, watch dull words like stuff, good, like, ok, etc. You want to expand your vocabulary to convince the interviewer you are intelligent and wordy. However, don't get too caught up and try to implement long fancy words that you barely know the meaning of or even how to pronounce.

Your appearance says a lot about you, and although interviewer are not supposed to discriminate in such a way, an interviewer will have a preference. Piercings should be taken out and masked so that they aren't noticable, tattoos should be covered up, shave before hand and perhaps consider getting a haircut, and avoid an overuse of makeup, especially darker colors. Try to dress in business formal, or business casual clothing and make sure it is professional, conservative, and not revealling.
